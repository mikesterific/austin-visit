Synopsis

Two old friends fly in from Florida after far too many years. Over one weekend they will walk a lakefront at golden hour, eat Texas barbecue to live psychedelic soul, swim in 68-degree spring water in the middle of a Texas July, watch the sun fall into Lake Travis with a house-brewed beer in hand, and — if all goes according to plan — two-step in an East Austin honky-tonk at midnight. There is no intermission. There is, however, beer.

House Notes

How Friday Played

July 17, 2026 — filed for the permanent record, so the cast can reread it later.

Act I is in the books. Fun was had by all. Old friendships were rekindled. Serendipity showed up without an invitation — and stayed for dinner.

Dinner at Lamberts

The meal was excellent. “The best I’ve ever had” got mentioned multiple times, which is the sort of line a stage manager writes down carefully. On stage: only two musicians, somehow with a great full sound that filled the room. Julie was a big fan — and tipped accordingly.

Parker Jazz Club

After dinner, the cast walked a few blocks for jazz as planned. They heard it along with the table behind them — a shared soundtrack, a little more communal than intended — and Julie really enjoyed it anyway.

The Hidden Speakeasy

Then the real late-night move: a hidden speakeasy for libations and the kind of conversation that needs a quieter room. After the music, they could finally hear each other — which, as it turns out, was the point of the whole reunion.

Plans were made that Matt and Julie will be retiring in Austin.

Curtain on Act I. Saturday waits in the wings — springs, hills, and whatever serendipity has left in the budget.

  2. 3:30–4:40 PM

    Scene 2 · Costume Change

    Back at the W Austin

    Shower, change, cool down, and avoid arriving at sunset dinner smelling like a municipal swimming facility. Leave the W around 4:40 PM to meet Mike at the brewery around 5:30 for the scenic-drive curtain call.

    Scenic drive to Oasis in Google Maps (via 360 / Pennybacker Bridge) → The pretty inbound: Capital of Texas Hwy, then RM 2222 west into the hills — about 45–55 minutes.

  3. 5:30–6:30 PM · a tighter loop

    Scene 3 · Mike’s Lake Travis Scenic Drive the local’s cut

    Meet at the OTXBC parking lot · ~45–60 minutes with the dam stop · returns in time for sunset

    “Before we eat, let me show you my favorite part of Austin.”

    This isn’t transportation — the drive is the attraction. A tight loop through the hills that makes the brewery arrival feel earned: Comanche Trail, Mansfield Dam, Hudson Bend, then back to the deck just as the light turns gold.

    ~5:30Leg 1 · North on Comanche Trail (~15 min) — one of the prettiest roads in Austin: winding Hill Country curves, Lake Travis appearing through the trees, custom homes tucked into the hills. No hurry.
    ~5:45Stop · Mansfield Dam overlook (10–15 min) — walk out, stretch legs, take photos. Guide’s talking points: Lake Travis feeding Lake Austin, the flood-control story, and how dramatically the water level swings.
    ~6:00Leg 2 · Loop through Hudson Bend (~15 min) — oak and cedar, lake views, a fresh angle instead of retracing the route. This is where visitors usually say “I had no idea Austin looked like this.”
    ~6:15Leg 3 · Hudson Bend back to the brewery (~10 min) — arrive about 6:25–6:30, timed so beer, decks, and shop-browsing lead straight into sunset.

    Open the loop in Google Maps (starts & ends at OTXBC) → A shorter loop — Comanche Trail, the dam, Hudson Bend, and home.

    Nearby options & fine print
    • Optional upgrade: Mansfield Dam Park sits right by the dam with a gazebo overlook and shoreline views. Worth it only if hours and day-use fee cooperate — check before peeling into the park.
    • Best leftover time: skip extra driving and wander the Oasis / OTXBC decks and shops when you get back — that’s the real pre-sunset payoff.
    • Skip: Hippie Hollow (clothing-optional), and farther parks like Arkansas Bend or Bob Wentz — same problem as Lakeway.
    • Conversation material: why Austin sprawled west into the hills, how the lake changes with rainfall, downtown vs. upscale west-side contrast, and Mike’s own favorite roads and memories.
    • Skip or shorten freely if thunderstorms or traffic intrude — the sunset is still the headliner.
  4. 6:45 PM → 9:00 PM

    6550 Comanche Trail #301 · 450 feet above Lake Travis · sunset at approximately 8:32 PM

Act II · Finale

The Sixth Street Double Feature

Depart the brewery ~9:00 PM · rideshare straight to historic Sixth Street · arrive 9:45–10:00. Both Sixths in one night: the neon chaos of the historic strip first, then a migration east across I-35 as the night matures. Not a rigid bar-by-bar military campaign — anchors and drift.

1

Opening Number · The Sixth Street Stroll

Historic Sixth Street · the neon strip · free

Start the night by walking the most famous strip in Austin nightlife, music pouring out of every open door. World-class people-watching, dueling bar bands, and no commitment — only walk into a room that earns it. The street itself is the show.

  • Rowdy, crowded, and more shot-bar chaos than curated listening — this is Austin’s id, not its concert hall. Twenty to forty minutes is the right dose.
  • If Hot Summer Nights is still running Saturday, the Red River district is two blocks north — check the festival calendar for a free detour.
2

Crossing · West Sixth to East Sixth

Under I-35 · a 10–15 minute walk or a five-minute rideshare

Head east and the volume drops while the quality rises: the shot bars give way to patios, listening rooms, and honky-tonks. This is the same street changing its costume between scenes.

3

Anchor · Hotel Vegas + Volstead Lounge

East Sixth near Chicon · connected neighboring venues

One complex, many rooms: live indie, garage, psychedelic, punk, or Latin-influenced bands at Hotel Vegas; DJs and dancing at Volstead; a busy courtyard in between. Sample everything without relocating the group.

  • Previously listed: “Fear and Loathing in Hotel Vegas” through the evening; Los Desechos ~10 PM; DJ Orión García at Volstead 10 PM–2 AM.
  • Reconfirm Saturday — third-party listings drift.

Friday

Warm, humid, mostly cloudy; possible afternoon or early-evening storms. The lake walk stays optional; dinner and nearly every music option are indoors.

Saturday

Hot and humid, with storm risk concentrated late afternoon into early evening and improvement possible at night. Check Barton Springs status and radar in the morning; keep the scenic drive flexible; OTXBC has indoor seating if clouds mute the sunset (though the brewery itself sometimes closes for severe weather — check before driving out). Transportation

Friday

Everything walks from the W: the lake, Lamberts, and Parker Jazz Club. Rideshare only if feet are done or weather turns.

Saturday

Wheels required: Barton Springs, the Lake Travis scenic drive, the brewery, East Sixth. Mike drives the scenic loop — that’s the point of it.

Strong recommendation: rideshare for the final stretch if everyone is drinking. Driving from the brewery to Sixth Street and then hunting parking is a poor closing act. The stroll from historic Sixth to East Sixth is walkable, or a five-minute rideshare under I-35 if feet are done.
