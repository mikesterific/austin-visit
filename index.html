Synopsis

Two old friends fly in from Florida after far too many years. Over one weekend they will walk a lakefront at golden hour, eat Texas barbecue to live psychedelic soul, swim in 68-degree spring water in the middle of a Texas July, watch the sun fall into Lake Travis with a house-brewed beer in hand, and — if all goes according to plan — two-step in an East Austin honky-tonk at midnight. There is no intermission. There is, however, beer.

Interlude

The Friday Night Route

After Lamberts, the plan is a route, not a menu: a free festival, the neon strip, and — if the tank allows — the east side. Nothing ticketed, everything abandonable, and five understudies waiting in the wings.

“We flew across the country today” “Unexplained airport adrenaline”

1

Starting Point · Hot Summer Nights

Red River Cultural District · Mohawk area · a short rideshare or ~20-minute walk from Lamberts

A free, multi-venue festival: wander the district, listen from the sidewalk, and step into whichever room sounds best — funk, rock, punk, and beyond. No tickets bought in advance means nothing wasted if the group fades early.

Festival info →

2

Then · The Sixth Street Stroll

Historic Sixth Street · two blocks south of Red River · free

Drift south from the festival and walk the most famous strip in Austin nightlife, music pouring out of every open door. World-class people-watching, and only walk into a bar that earns it — the street itself is the show.

Rowdy, crowded, and more shot-bar chaos than curated listening — this is Austin's id, not its concert hall.

3

Energy Permitting · East Sixth optional finale

Across I-35 · a short walk or five-minute rideshare east

Keep heading east under the highway and the volume drops while the quality rises: patios, serious cocktails at Whisler’s, and honky-tonk country at The White Horse. Consider it a location scout for Saturday’s grand finale — a taste tonight, the full production tomorrow.

The escape hatch is always open: a rideshare back to the W takes ten minutes from anywhere on the route.

Act II

Springs, Hills & Sunset

Saturday, July 18 — The full Austin production. Hot, humid, and worth every degree.

  1. 1:30–3:30 PM

    Scene 1 · Barton Springs Pool

    2131 William Barton Drive · Zilker Park

    Three acres of spring-fed water holding steady at 68–70°F — the single best answer to a Texas July afternoon. Swim, float, and let Florida and Texas call a truce.

    Stage directions & props
    • Bring: swimsuit, towel, water, sunscreen, sandals, and a dry bag for phones.
    • Pool hours generally 5:00 AM–10:00 PM; guarded swimming from 8:00 AM.
    • Barton Springs reopened after a June 2026 flooding closure and is currently listed as open — confirm status Saturday morning.
    • Official pool status →
  2. 3:30–4:40 PM

    Scene 2 · Costume Change

    Back at the W Austin

    Shower, change, cool down, and avoid arriving at sunset dinner smelling like a municipal swimming facility. Leave the W around 4:40 PM to meet Mike at the brewery around 5:30 for the scenic-drive curtain call.

  3. 5:30–6:40 PM · 60-minute core loop

    Scene 3 · Mike’s Lake Travis Scenic Drive the local’s cut

    Meet at the OTXBC parking lot · 60–70 minutes with the dam stop · returns in time for sunset

    “Before we eat, let me show you my favorite part of Austin.”

    This isn’t transportation — the drive is the attraction. A slow loop through the hills that makes the brewery arrival feel earned: city, then Hill Country, then Lake Travis, then back to the deck just as the light turns gold.

    ~5:30Leg 1 · North on Comanche Trail (~15 min) — one of the prettiest roads in Austin: winding Hill Country curves, Lake Travis appearing through the trees, custom homes tucked into the hills. No hurry.
    ~5:45Stop · Mansfield Dam overlook (10–15 min) — walk out, stretch legs, take photos. Guide’s talking points: Lake Travis feeding Lake Austin, the flood-control story, and how dramatically the water level swings.
    ~6:00Leg 2 · West to Lakeway (~10–15 min) — golf communities, oak and cedar, lake views whenever they appear. This is where visitors usually say “I had no idea Austin looked like this.”
    ~6:15Leg 3 · Loop back through Hudson Bend (~15 min) — a fresh angle on the lake instead of retracing the route, with Austin’s boom and boating culture as material.
    ~6:30Leg 4 · Hudson Bend back to the brewery (~10 min) — arrive about 6:40, timed so beer, decks, and shop-browsing lead straight into sunset.
    The guide’s monologue & fine print
    • Conversation material: why Austin sprawled west into the hills, how the lake changes with rainfall, the “Keep Austin Weird” downtown vs. upscale Lakeway/Westlake contrast, and Mike’s own favorite roads and memories.
    • Optional pit stop in Lakeway for coffee, ice cream, or a cold drink — only if something catches the eye. It turns the loop into the 75-minute version, so skip it if sunset-deck time matters more.
    • Why it works: guests arrive at the brewery feeling like they’ve explored what makes Lake Travis special, not like they’ve pulled up to a restaurant.
    • Skip or shorten freely if thunderstorms or traffic intrude — the sunset is still the headliner.
  4. 6:45 PM → 9:00 PM

    Scene 4 · Oasis Texas Brewing Company

    6550 Comanche Trail #301 · 450 feet above Lake Travis · sunset at approximately 8:32 PM

    A lakeside brewery and restaurant perched high over Lake Travis: house-brewed beer, a locally sourced menu (the Pimento Cheese Burger has a fan club), indoor and outdoor seating across multiple levels, and a sunset view that rivals any in the Hill Country.

    6:45–7:00Arrive, claim a lake-view spot, order the first round of house beer
    7:00–8:15Dinner and drinks
    8:15–8:50Sunset and deck time — the show within the show
    ~9:00Depart for East Sixth
    • Saturday hours: taproom noon–10 PM, kitchen noon–9 PM — dinner fits comfortably before the kitchen closes.
    • OTXBC takes reservations on OpenTable — booking ahead beats hoping; either way, arrive before sunset for the best deck spots.
    • Hours can shift with weather — confirm Saturday via their site or social media.
    • Parking: dirt lot plus a three-floor public garage.

Program Notes

Weather Strategy

Friday

Warm, humid, mostly cloudy; possible afternoon or early-evening storms. The lake walk stays optional; dinner and nearly every music option are indoors.

Saturday

Hot and humid, with storm risk concentrated late afternoon into early evening and improvement possible at night. Check Barton Springs status and radar in the morning; keep the scenic drive flexible; OTXBC has indoor seating if clouds mute the sunset (though the brewery itself sometimes closes for severe weather — check before driving out). East Sixth survives all weather.

forecast.weather.gov →

Transportation

Friday

Everything walks from the W: the lake, Lamberts, and most downtown venues. The Friday route’s starting point in the Red River district is a ~20-minute walk or a short rideshare from Lamberts; rideshare is also the move for The 04 Center or a late hop across I-35.

Saturday

Wheels required: Barton Springs, the Lake Travis scenic drive, the brewery, East Sixth. Mike drives the scenic loop — that’s the point of it.

Strong recommendation: rideshare for the final stretch if everyone is drinking. Driving from the brewery to East Sixth and then hunting parking is a poor closing act.

A Note for the Guests

Friday: After checking in, take an optional walk along Lady Bird Lake, then meet for our 6:15 dinner reservation at Lamberts. A local psychedelic-soul and funk band will be playing, and afterward the plan is a wander: the free Hot Summer Nights festival in the Red River district, then a neon stroll down the legendary Sixth Street, and — if we still have energy — a peek at East Sixth. If we’d rather sit down, jazz, blues, folk, or a full-scale concert are all easy substitutes.

Saturday: We’ll swim at Barton Springs, return to the hotel to reset, then take a scenic drive around Lake Travis — Hill Country roads, the Mansfield Dam overlook, Lakeway, and Hudson Bend — before dinner and house-brewed beer at Oasis Texas Brewing Company for the Lake Travis sunset. Afterward, we’ll explore East Sixth for live music, cocktails, DJs, and finish with country music and dancing at The White Horse.

What to pack: Smart casual for Friday dinner (no jacket or tie needed — this is Austin), plus a swimsuit, sandals, and comfortable casual summer clothes for Saturday. Comfortable walking shoes matter more than style points all weekend.

— Your host, who has been looking forward to this for a very long time
